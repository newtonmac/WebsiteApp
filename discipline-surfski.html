Surfski

What is Surfski?

A narrow, sit-on-top kayak designed for open ocean paddling. Evolved from lifesaving boats in South Africa and Australia. Paddled with a double-blade paddle. Built for speed, downwind runs, and ocean racing. The ultimate open-water craft — fast, exhilarating, and demanding.

Types of Surfski

Entry-Level / Stability Surfski

Wider beam (~50-52cm), forgiving, and great for learning. Examples: Epic V5, Think Evo II.

Intermediate Surfski

Medium beam (~46-48cm), offering a balance of speed and stability. Examples: Epic V7, Fenn Swordfish.

Elite / Racing Surfski

Narrow (~42-44cm), the fastest option, designed for experienced paddlers. Examples: Epic V10, Fenn Elite, Nelo 560.

Double Surfski (S2)

Two-person surfski, great for training partners and shared ocean adventures.

Essential Gear

Surfski

The boat itself. $2,500 - $8,000+

Wing Paddle

Carbon wing paddle for efficient forward stroke. $200 - $600

PFD (Life Jacket)

Low-profile personal flotation device. $50 - $150

Leash

Critical for ocean paddling — keeps you connected to the boat. $20 - $40

Hydration System

Stay hydrated during long open-water sessions.

GPS Watch

Track speed, distance, and routes. $300 - $500

Getting Started

  • Learn to remount — you WILL fall off.
  • Start on flat water.
  • Practice bracing and balance.
  • Join a surfski club for group paddles.
  • Work up to downwind runs gradually.
  • Swim fitness is essential as backup.

Who Is It For?

Fit paddlers seeking speed and ocean thrills. Triathletes, ocean swimmers, and kayakers looking for the next challenge. Not ideal for true beginners (start with a wider model).
