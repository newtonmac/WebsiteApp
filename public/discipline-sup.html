Stand Up Paddleboarding (SUP)

What is SUP?

Stand up paddleboarding (SUP) involves standing on a large, buoyant board and propelling yourself across the water with a single-bladed paddle. Originating in Hawaii, SUP has grown into one of the most popular water sports in the world. It is incredibly versatile — you can use it for fitness, racing, surfing, touring long distances, or even practicing yoga on the water. Whether you are on a calm lake, a flowing river, or ocean swells, SUP offers something for everyone.

Types of SUP Boards

All-Around

Typically 10'6" to 11' in length. These boards are wide, stable, and great for beginners. They handle a variety of conditions and are the most popular choice for first-time paddlers.

Touring

Ranging from 12'6" to 14', touring boards are longer and narrower than all-arounds. They track straighter and glide faster, making them ideal for covering longer distances on flatwater.

Racing

Also 12'6" to 14', racing SUPs are narrow and built purely for speed. They require more balance and skill but reward experienced paddlers with exceptional performance on the water.

Inflatable

Inflatable SUPs are highly portable and easy to store, making them a great option for travelers or those with limited space. Modern inflatables are remarkably rigid and perform well for recreational use.

Wetsuit / Rashguard Seasonal protection from cold or sun $40 – $200

Getting Started

  • Start on calm, flat water such as a sheltered lake or bay.
  • Begin by kneeling on the board to find your balance, then slowly stand up.
  • Keep your eyes on the horizon, not on your feet — this helps with stability.
  • Paddle on both sides to travel in a straight line, switching every few strokes.
  • Consider taking a beginner lesson to learn proper technique and safety basics.

Who Is It For?

Stand up paddleboarding is for anyone. It has one of the lowest barriers to entry of any water sport and is suitable for all ages and fitness levels. SUP provides a fantastic full-body workout, engaging your core, legs, arms, and back. Whether you want a peaceful paddle at sunrise, an intense racing session, or a fun family outing on the water, SUP delivers.
