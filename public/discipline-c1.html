Sprint Canoe (C1)

What is C1?

A single-person sprint canoe for Olympic flatwater racing. The paddler kneels on one knee using a single-blade paddle. Returned to the full Olympic program in 2020 with women's events. Incredibly technical — requires power, balance, and blade control.

Types of Sprint Canoe

C1 (Solo)

Single-person sprint canoe, approximately 5.2m long. Paddled with a single-blade paddle from a kneeling position.

C2 (Two-Person)

Two-person sprint canoe, approximately 6.5m long. Both paddlers kneel and paddle on opposite sides.

C4 (Four-Person)

Four-person sprint canoe. A powerful team boat requiring exceptional coordination.

Para Canoe

VL1-VL3 classifications for adaptive athletes. Modified seating and support for inclusive competition.

Essential Gear

C1 Boat

Carbon/kevlar construction for racing performance. $2,000 - $6,000+

Single-Blade Canoe Paddle

Purpose-built for the canoe sprint stroke. $150 - $500

Kneeling Block / Pad

Custom fitted to the paddler for comfort and stability in the kneeling position.

Spray Deck

Keeps water out of the cockpit during racing. $40 - $80

PFD (Race-Spec)

Minimal, race-specification personal flotation device.

Getting Started

  • Find a club with a canoe sprint program.
  • The kneeling position takes time to learn.
  • Start in a wider training boat.
  • Focus on single-side paddling technique — you don't switch sides in C1.
  • Core and hip flexibility are crucial.

Who Is It For?

Technical athletes who love precision. Popular in Eastern Europe, Cuba, Canada, and Germany. Growing in the US. Great for paddlers who want an Olympic pathway with fewer competitors than K1.
