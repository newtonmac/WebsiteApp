Dragon Boat Racing

What is Dragon Boat Racing?

Dragon boat racing is a team paddling sport using long, ornate boats decorated with a dragon head and tail. The sport originated in China over 2,000 years ago and has grown into a global phenomenon. Each crew consists of 20 paddlers, 1 drummer who sets the pace, and 1 steersperson who guides the boat. Races are sprint-format — 200m, 500m, and 2000m — on flat water, making it one of the most exciting and accessible paddle sports in the world.

Types of Dragon Boats

Standard (20-Paddler)

IDBF Spec — Length: 12.5m

The standard competition dragon boat, built to International Dragon Boat Federation (IDBF) specifications. Used in sanctioned races worldwide, from local regattas to world championships.

Small Boat (10-Paddler)

A smaller dragon boat popular for corporate events, community races, and teams with fewer members. Same excitement, half the crew — a great entry point for new teams and organizations.

Festival Boats

Heavier, more durable boats designed for recreational and festival events. Built to handle high-volume use by beginners and casual paddlers at community celebrations and charity races.

Essential Gear

Boats are provided by clubs and teams — you do not need to own one.

Personal Paddle (IDBF Spec)

A dragon boat paddle built to IDBF specifications. Many competitive paddlers invest in their own carbon or fiberglass paddle for a customized fit and feel.

$80 – $300

PFD (Personal Flotation Device)

A low-profile life vest required at most practices and all races. Choose one that allows free arm movement for paddling.

$50 – $100

Seat Pad

A cushioned pad for the bench seat. Dragon boat benches are hard — a good seat pad makes a big difference during long practices and race days.

$15 – $30

Team Uniform / Jersey

Most teams have matching jerseys for race day. Your team will typically organize a group order once you join.

Water Bottle

Stay hydrated between races and during practice. Bring a bottle that seals securely — it will be on the boat with you.

Getting Started

  • Join a local dragon boat team — no experience needed. Most teams actively recruit new paddlers and welcome all backgrounds.
  • All fitness levels are welcome. Dragon boat teams range from casual recreational crews to elite competitive squads. There is a place for everyone.
  • Practices are on the water 2–3 times per week. Expect a mix of technique drills, race pieces, and endurance work.
  • Learn to paddle in sync with the drumbeat. The drummer sets the rhythm — your job is to match it. Timing as a crew is everything in dragon boat racing.

Who Is It For?

Dragon boat racing is for team-oriented people, corporate groups, and anyone who loves the energy of a big crew sport. It has a particularly strong community among cancer survivor teams — breast cancer dragon boat paddling is a global movement with hundreds of teams worldwide. Whether you are looking for fitness, camaraderie, or competition, dragon boat racing delivers all three.
