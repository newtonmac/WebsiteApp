Canoeing

What is Canoeing?

Canoeing is paddling an open-top boat with a single-bladed paddle, either kneeling or sitting. It is one of the oldest forms of watercraft, with roots stretching back thousands of years. Canoes are used for recreation, racing, wilderness tripping, and fishing.

Canoeing has a deep North American tradition. Indigenous peoples developed the canoe as a vital means of travel and trade. It played a central role in the fur trade era and has since evolved into a beloved modern sport enjoyed by millions on lakes, rivers, and waterways around the world.

Types of Canoes

Recreational Canoe

15' to 17' in length. Wide, stable, and family-friendly. Ideal for calm lakes, slow rivers, and casual day trips. A great starting point for beginners.

Solo Canoe

12' to 16' in length. Lighter and easier to handle for a single paddler. Perfect for those who want to explore at their own pace.

Tandem / Tripping Canoe

16' to 18'6" in length. High carrying capacity designed for multi-day wilderness trips. Built to handle portaging with gear for extended adventures.

Racing Canoe (C1 / C2)

Narrow and lightweight, built for speed. Used in sprint and marathon racing disciplines. Requires strong technique and balance.

Whitewater Canoe

Shorter hull with extra rocker for manoeuvrability. Designed for river running and navigating rapids. Often outfitted with airbags and thigh straps.

Getting Started

  • Learn the J-stroke early. It lets you paddle straight on one side without constantly switching, and it is the hallmark of good canoeing technique.
  • Practice getting in and out of the canoe on calm, shallow water. Step into the centre line and keep your weight low.
  • Always keep your centre of gravity low. Kneel or sit on the seat with a wide, stable stance.
  • Start with a recreational canoe on a calm lake before progressing to rivers or longer trips.
  • Consider joining a guided canoe trip. Experienced guides will teach you strokes, safety, and camp craft in a supportive setting.

Who Is It For?

Canoeing is for wilderness enthusiasts, families, anglers, marathon racers, and anyone who loves spending time on lakes and rivers. Whether you are looking for a peaceful morning paddle, a multi-day backcountry trip, or the thrill of competitive racing, there is a style of canoeing that fits.

Canoe camping and wilderness tripping remain among the best outdoor experiences you can have. Load your gear, push off from shore, and let the waterway lead the way.
