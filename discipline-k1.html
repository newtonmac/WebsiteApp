Sprint Kayak (K1)

What is K1?

A single-person sprint kayak used in Olympic flatwater racing. Extremely narrow, unstable, and fast. Paddled with a double-blade wing paddle on flat water courses (200m, 500m, 1000m). One of the oldest Olympic paddle sports (since 1936).

Types of Sprint Kayak

K1 (Solo)

Single-person sprint kayak, approximately 5.2m long with a maximum weight of 12kg. The standard Olympic class.

K2 (Two-Person)

Two-person sprint kayak, approximately 6.5m long. Requires precise synchronisation between paddlers.

K4 (Four-Person)

Four-person sprint kayak, approximately 11m long. The ultimate team boat for flatwater racing.

Para Kayak

KL1-KL3 classifications for adaptive athletes. Modified cockpits and support systems for inclusive competition.

Essential Gear

K1 Boat

Carbon/kevlar construction for maximum performance. $2,000 - $6,000+

Wing Paddle

Designed for the forward stroke technique used in sprint kayaking. $200 - $600

Spray Deck

Keeps water out of the cockpit during racing. $40 - $80

PFD (Race-Specific)

Thin, low-profile personal flotation device designed for racing.

Cockpit Sponge

For bailing water that gets into the cockpit during training and racing.

GPS / Stroke Rate Monitor

Track performance metrics including speed, distance, and stroke rate.

Getting Started

  • Join a canoe/kayak club with a sprint program.
  • Start in a wider training K1 or recreational kayak.
  • Focus on balance first — expect to capsize often.
  • Learn proper wing paddle technique.
  • Core strength and rotation are key.

Who Is It For?

Competitive athletes, Olympic hopefuls, and fitness paddlers who love measurable performance. Requires access to flat water (lakes, rivers, regatta courses). Youth programs are a great entry point.
