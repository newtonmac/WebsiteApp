About This Project

EV Route Planner was built by an EV owner who wanted to get the most out of every charge. Realizing that elevation plays a huge role in battery consumption, I set out to build a tool that factors in terrain — not just distance — when planning routes, helping you drive as efficiently as possible.

The idea is simple: two routes can be the same distance, but the one that climbs a mountain will drain your battery far more than the one that takes a gentler path. This app analyzes elevation profiles, calculates grade-aware energy consumption for your specific vehicle, and helps you pick the route that saves the most battery — so you can drive with confidence instead of worry.

This is a free community project, built for EV drivers by an EV driver. If you find it useful or have ideas to make it better, I'd love to hear from you.

Technology: Built with Google Maps Platform APIs including Maps JavaScript API, Places API, Directions API, and Elevation API. Vehicle efficiency data is based on publicly available EPA ratings and manufacturer specifications. Energy calculations use a grade-aware model that accounts for motor efficiency, regenerative braking, and terrain profile.
